wild mushroom risotto

Wild Mushroom Risotto is a creamy, comforting dish featuring earthy mushrooms and a rich, velvety rice base.

With simple ingredients like shallots, garlic, and Parmesan, it brings out deep, umami flavors, perfect for a cozy meal or special occasion.

Comforting and earthy, this wild mushroom risotto combines creamy rice with savory mushrooms.

first steps:

Before you start cooking the wild mushroom risotto, there are a few important steps to prepare. First, clean and slice your mushrooms — you can use a mix of wild mushrooms for a richer flavor profile.

You’ll need to prep your broth, either vegetable or chicken, and keep it warm on a low simmer while you cook.

Additionally, get your pan ready for sautéing the onions and garlic, and measure out the rice and other ingredients so everything is within arm’s reach.

This helps keep the cooking process smooth and ensures you’re ready for the next step when it’s time to add ingredients.

Finally, make sure you have a wooden spoon or spatula for stirring the risotto — constant stirring is key to achieving the creamy texture.

Here’s a quick checklist to get you set:

  • Prep your ingredients:

    Clean and slice your wild mushrooms for a richer flavor mix. Measure out your rice and other ingredients.

  • Prepare your broth

    Heat your vegetable or chicken broth and keep it warm on low simmer while you cook.

  • Have a stirring spoon ready:

    Grab a wooden spoon or spatula to stir the risotto for that creamy texture.

  • Get your pan ready:

    Heat your pan for sautéing onions and garlic, making sure all your tools are ready.

you will need:

  • 1 cup wild mushrooms, sliced (mix for richer flavor)
  • 1 cup arborio rice (for creamy texture)
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth (kept warm)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp butter (for sautéing)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/4 cup white wine (optional, for depth of flavor)
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

    • optional:

  • Fresh thyme or parsley for garnish

time to cook!

  1. Begin by heating your broth in a saucepan over low heat, keeping it warm as you cook the risotto.
  2. In a large pan, heat some olive oil over medium heat. Add your onions and sauté until translucent, about 2-3 minutes.
  3. Add your garlic and cook for another 30 seconds until fragrant, then toss in the sliced mushrooms. Cook for 5-7 minutes until the mushrooms soften and release their juices.
  4. Stir in the rice, cooking for 1-2 minutes until it’s lightly toasted and coated with the oil and mushroom mixture.
  5. Begin adding your warm broth, one ladleful at a time, stirring constantly. Let the rice absorb the liquid before adding more broth. Continue this process for about 18-20 minutes, or until the rice is creamy and tender.
  6. Once the rice is done, stir in the grated Parmesan and butter. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve the risotto hot, garnished with fresh herbs like parsley or thyme, and enjoy your wild mushroom risotto!

additional tips:

Wild mushroom risotto is a comforting and versatile dish, perfect for elevating your weeknight dinners or special occasions.

The mix of earthy mushrooms, creamy rice, and rich Parmesan creates a satisfying texture and depth of flavor.

You can use any variety of mushrooms—cremini, shiitake, or porcini—depending on what you have available or what you enjoy most.

If you prefer a vegetarian version, simply substitute vegetable broth for the chicken broth and use vegan butter and cheese alternatives.

For an extra boost of flavor, try adding a drizzle of truffle oil or a sprinkle of fresh herbs like thyme or rosemary when serving.
