pad see ew

Pad see ew is a classic Thai stir-fried noodle dish featuring wide rice noodles, savory soy sauce, and a combination of tender meat or tofu, egg, and vibrant vegetables.

The smoky, charred flavor comes from quickly cooking the ingredients on high heat, often in a wok, making it a quick yet deliciously satisfying meal.

Stir-fried noodles, savory sauce, and a crispy egg.

first steps:

This dish comes together fast, so it’s important to have all your ingredients prepped and ready to go.

Make sure to have your wok or large frying pan heated up and your protein and vegetables ready for stir-frying.

It’s also a good idea to have your noodles cooked and drained before starting.

Here’s a quick checklist to get you set:

  • Prep your ingredients:

    Chop your vegetables (e.g., broccoli, bell pepper, or carrots), and slice your protein (chicken, beef, or tofu)

  • Cook your noodles:

    Make sure your wide rice noodles are cooked and drained, then set them aside.

  • Heat your wok:

    Get your wok or large pan hot and ready for stir-frying.

you will need:

  • 200g wide rice noodles (stir-fried texture)
  • 1 chicken breast (or tofu for vegetarian option), sliced
  • 1 cup broccoli florets (or a mix of your favorite veggies)
  • 1 bell pepper, thinly sliced
  • 1 small carrot, julienned
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 3 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 tbsp oil (vegetable or peanut oil for high heat)

    • optional:

  • Chili flakes or fresh sliced chili for heat!

time to cook!

  1. Begin by cooking your rice noodles according to the package instructions. Once cooked, drain and set aside. You want them slightly firm so they don’t become mushy when stir-frying.
  2. In a large pan or wok, heat your oil over medium-high heat. Add the protein (chicken, tofu, or your choice) and cook until browned and cooked through. Set the protein aside.
  3. In the same pan, add more oil if needed and toss in your garlic, cooking for about 30 seconds until fragrant. Add the vegetables (broccoli, bell pepper, and carrot) and stir-fry for 3-4 minutes, until they begin to soften but remain crisp.
  4. Push the vegetables to the side of the pan, crack the egg into the empty space, and scramble it until cooked. Once scrambled, mix it with the vegetables.
  5. Add the cooked noodles back into the pan with the veggies and egg. Pour in your sauce mixture (soy sauce, oyster sauce, sugar) and stir everything together, ensuring the noodles are well coated.
  6. Toss the cooked protein back in and give everything one final stir. Let the dish cook for another minute, allowing all the flavors to meld together.
  7. Serve hot with a sprinkle of chili flakes for some extra heat, and enjoy your homemade Pad See Ew!

additional tips:

Pad See Ew is a simple yet flavorful dish that’s perfect for a busy weeknight or when you're craving something comforting.

The combination of tender noodles, crispy veggies, and savory sauce hits all the right notes.

Whether you're using chicken, tofu, or a mix of both, this dish is adaptable to suit your tastes.

For an extra kick, don't hesitate to add some chili flakes or a squeeze of lime when serving.

With just a few ingredients and minimal prep, you'll have a satisfying meal that brings the taste of Thai street food right to your kitchen.
